Three British and one New Zealand telecom workers were kidnapped after a shootout between their bodyguards and the attackers . One bodyguard and an unknown number of attackers were wounded in the abduction in the breakaway Chechnya region . Kidnapping , generally for ransom , has become a growth industry for Chechen rebels with 176 abducted this year . Most kidnappings are for ransom and 90 of the victims have been released . The four foreigners were not so fortunate ; their severed heads were found two months later after a lengthy search . Chechen authorities called the deaths a major blow to efforts for independence and blamed `` outside forces '' and their Chechen henchmen .
