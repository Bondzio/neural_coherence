<s> Hi I'm looking into doing precisely the same thing ... trying to get a DVD burner into my Mac Mini. </s>
<s> It's not difficult! </s>
<s> 1. </s>
<s> Ebay has lots of the right DVD burners for sale ... they are new ... just do a search (I'm tracking several of these right now and will probably go for the Pioneer since I've got Pioneer DVD burners in all my Macs and they work beautifully). </s>
<s> 2. </s>
<s> Make sure you're comfortable with opening the Mini and working in tight spaces (I cut my teeth on Mac SE's and SE/30's so I'm quite comfy with this). </s>
<s> To prepare myself for this, I took it apart and replaced the RAM in my Mac Mini Core Solo. </s>
<s> This is different from the Mac Mini PowerPC version ... the RAM is more difficult to get to in the Intel Mini's! </s>
<s> There is plenty of help available on the internet on how to open the Mini and how to get to the various parts. </s>
<s> The optical drive has an interface card attached directly to the back. </s>
<s> This interface card plugs into a slot on the motherboard. </s>
<s> As long as you take care when taking the machine apart and putting it together again, it is not difficult to replace the optical drive. </s>
<s> Just take your time and don't be distracted by anything else. </s>
<s> Good luck! </s>
