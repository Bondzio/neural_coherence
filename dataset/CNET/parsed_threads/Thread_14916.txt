<s> Welcome to the PC world. </s>
<s> It's a place where hardware and data just vanish. </s>
<s> It drives people crazy...now you see it/now you don't. </s>
<s> #1 rule.....backup/backup/backup. </s>
<s> Did you violate the rule? </s>
<s> As far as I know HD's don't go click-click-click. </s>
<s> So unless someone has a better thought, I would install that new HD, load windows, install the old HD as a slave and see if I could salvage any of MY STUFF. </s>
<s> I suggest you visit the HD makers support website and get thier diagnostic test pgm.. Use it to test but don't use the &quot;destructive type&quot; that will wipe out data, there are other tests for a quick check. </s>
<s> As the other poster mentioned, you have a new HD, install as prime or master(alone)and go from there. </s>
<s> Then later add old HD as suggested to try to recover data. </s>
<s> You can swap power leads for HD as well to see if that helps(probably not) but heck its a shot. </s>
<s> Further, try the safemode booting and see just how far it gets, if it doesn't do that you probably do have a bad HD regardless. </s>
<s> if it does get pass and boot-up use the &quot;scan&quot; feature to quick check the HD or just back-up any data you can. </s>
<s> But, a click-click sound isn't good news, IMHO, be ready to install HD. </s>
