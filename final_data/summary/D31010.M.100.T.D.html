A killer cold wave hit much of Europe in November 1998 , lasting more than four weeks in some areas . Romania and Poland suffered most . France , Bulgaria , Italy , Croatia and Russia also experienced bitter cold . Temperatures reached as low as minus 26 degrees Celsius . In Romania cold and blizzard conditions closed major roadways , and dozens of trains were canceled . Communities were without electricity . The death toll was staggering . At least 39 died in Moscow . The death toll in Poland reached 85 in the four weeks after 16 November . Some died in storm-related accidents . Most victims were homeless , or those suffering from alcoholism .
