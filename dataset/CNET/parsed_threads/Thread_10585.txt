<s> While I agree that Cisco makes some of the best wireless hardware on the market, I believe it would be overkill for a home WLAN. </s>
<s> The average home user isn't worried about hackers in a sense where they would require LEAP. </s>
<s> Securing any wireless router with WEP should be good enough for the average home user. </s>
<s> Linksys is a division of Cisco that makes home networking hardware and their SRX router is probably one of the best home offerings I have found. </s>
<s> it offers a great range and compatibility with existing hardware as well is MIMO technology to reduce lag on a multi-computer environment. </s>
<s> Remember when you get something like pre-n from belkin you have to get adapters that are compatible as well. </s>
<s> Also remember that Internet only goes so fast, while you can transfer from you computer to your router at 108-130 MBpS the internet still only goes to the router at a max of 5-10 MBpS. </s>
<s> Depending on the number of computers you have and the type of internet service, you may be able to get by (very well I may add) without the added cost of &quot;cutting edge&quot; wireless protocols. </s>
<s> I still use my linksys wireless g router and d-link desktop cards in my setup and it works great. </s>
