<s> I have a college degree in computer science (including N+ class) and I use the D-Link DI-704P (the black one, not the brown one) on my dsl with three computers at home. </s>
<s> A hub isn't really for connecting to the internet, rather for connecting segments of a network together. </s>
<s> Some hubs only have ports to connect computers together in a peer-to-peer environment, not to another source such as a dsl modem. </s>
<s> What hub are you using, and can you tell me what ports you are using? </s>
<s> Also, I can tell you a router is much faster than a hub. </s>
<s> A router remembers what is plugged into each port, a hub just broadcasts the data to every machine that is connected to it. </s>
<s> If you have a packet for computer #1, instead of the hub saying this is for #1 so I'll send it there, it plays &quot;mail call&quot; like you see in old war movies. </s>
<s> &quot;I have mail for computer #1! </s>
<s> !&quot; &quot;OK, I'm over here at MAC address XX-XX-XX-XX-XX-XX.&quot; &quot;Alright. </s>
<s> Here comes your mail&quot;. </s>
<s> A router says &quot;I've delivered to computer #1 before, so I'll just use the same connection and send it there again&quot;. </s>
<s> Which would you say is faster? </s>
<s> Plus, most routers have a firewall built in (the D-Link DI-704P does). </s>
<s> A hardware firewall is MUCH better than ANY software firewall. </s>
<s> Why? </s>
<s> A hardware stops attacks/probes/unsolicited packets BEFORE it arrives at your computer, even before it gets into your network. </s>
<s> Where is a software firewall stored? </s>
<s> Inside your machine. </s>
<s> Any unwanted data much reach a point INSIDE your machine before it is stopped. </s>
<s> To some degree, it has already infiltrated into your computer. </s>
<s> And it's totally already on your network. </s>
<s> Go with the router, and you'll be better off. </s>
