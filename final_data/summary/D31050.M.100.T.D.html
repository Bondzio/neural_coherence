In October 1988 China signed the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights . Perhaps emboldened by that , dissidents pushed for the recognition of a new China Democratic Party . The government , citing a long-standing policy , said that it would not tolerate another political party . Three prominent dissidents , one of them Wu Xenli , working to establish the new party were arrested . They were accused of endangering national security , and faced prison terms of five years . Prospective defense lawyers were intimidated . The United States criticized the arrests , and an embassy official was rebuffed when he tried to observe a trial .
