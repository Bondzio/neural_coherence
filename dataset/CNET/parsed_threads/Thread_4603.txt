<s> Well, I've had this happen, too, if you're referring to the computer coming out of sleep mode some time during the night. </s>
<s> I assume that you're not talking about it actually turning itself on after being powered off, in that case it suggests sleepwalking *smile*. </s>
<s> What I've done for the problem of my computer coming out of sleep mode when I didn't do anything to cause it, you've already done some of. </s>
<s> When you disabled the &quot;wake on&quot; settings, that's a start. </s>
<s> There are also wake-on settings associated with your network driver that are accessible via the device manager, and you also need to disable wakeup via USB devices (mouse and keyboard). </s>
<s> You can find these settings, too, if you search for the them. </s>
<s> They're also available in the device manager under &quot;mouse&quot; and &quot;keyboard&quot; when running it as admin, in the &quot;Power Management&quot; tab for those devices. </s>
<s> Once you've turned off the ability of the network, and keyboard and mouse to wake your computer from sleep, you'll be good. </s>
<s> Then, in order to wake your computer from sleep, you'll just need a quick press of the power button, and that's the only thing that will wake your computer. </s>
<s> That has worked for me with several computers that seemed to like to wake up due to USB HID devices. </s>
<s> I fought this for months. </s>
<s> I even quit using &quot;sleep&quot; for a time! </s>
<s> Everything possible was checked, but it still woke up about 1: AM. </s>
<s> It finally ended up being the Microsoft clock (Win 7). </s>
<s> I changed the &quot;check&quot; time to noon, when I am usually using the system. </s>
<s> Been fine ever since! </s>
