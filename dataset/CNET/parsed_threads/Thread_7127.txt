<s> Robert, provides good sage advice. </s>
<s> However, if you want to play and the rewards may not be all that good once the system is running, proceed on. </s>
<s> First, that AMD cpu could be kaput as that era cpu just didn't like to be overheated and due to wear&tear it may have simply wore out. </s>
<s> Though, it is also prone to surge damage. </s>
<s> Next, though you have replaced the PSU, be sure it is a good one. </s>
<s> NOW, do all the things you need to do that you haven't already, place basic system on cardboard. </s>
<s> Remove all drives, except FD. </s>
<s> Be sure you have it all hooked up and try to power-ON with a bootable floppy(W9x version or any other OS). </s>
<s> If same results, remove cpu/HSF in its entirely. </s>
<s> Does it beep now?, if not, then mtrbd. </s>
<s> is shot. </s>
<s> If it beeps, then cpu or setup is shot. </s>
<s> If it beeps after you replace cpu back in with refreshed HSF, then socket or glitchy cpu. </s>
<s> Basically, it boils down to a too old setup or having to replace parts provided you find them. </s>
<s> Again, if nothing works or just no display, then think a video problem and try another video card(a basic PCI VGA one) and check results. </s>
<s> You may want to try that first as build-in video port could have pooped-out. </s>
<s> Also, even though you haven;'t found bad caps, these can be tricky to locate to include a true bad cap that simply failed and not the &quot;known manufactured poor quality&quot; ones that bulge, leak or blow-up&quot;. </s>
<s> What all this results is a lot of work to find the real cause if at all. </s>
<s> Sometimes, it a &quot;combination&quot; of all parts that no longer have the phmmf to get things done but on another system setup may. </s>
