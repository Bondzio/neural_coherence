<s> Another magic marketing number is all the 54m is. </s>
<s> When you read the fine print you get this rate only in a fairly controlled environment. </s>
<s> Before you worry a lot about these numbers the larger question is does it actually affect your performance. </s>
<s> It is highly unlikely you have a internet connection that is anywhere close to your wireless speeds. </s>
<s> In general the signal strength does not represent the quality of the signal. </s>
<s> You can have a very strong but noisy signal. </s>
<s> When the wireless connection gets errors above a certain rate it will select a slower rate even though the signal strength does not vary. </s>
<s> The amount of errors before it slows down varies between manufactures and also depends if it is the router or the PC card seeing the errors. </s>
<s> The errors assuming no defective hardware are caused by interference. </s>
<s> This could be anything from your neighbor, a cell phone etc etc. </s>
<s> It also can be as simple as something new in the house changing the reflection pattern and causing the signal to interfere with itself. </s>
<s> People just walking though a room can also change the signal quality. </s>
<s> Also having multiple machines on the same router greatly increases the interference which is why you can never get close to sharing full wireless bandwidth. </s>
<s> I would first try to change the channels you use maybe other will work better. </s>
<s> Different frequency have different interference patterns and if the source is external you may avoid it. </s>
<s> Also lock all your devices to G just a one B device will slow the rest down. </s>
<s> You could also try moving the router to a slightly different location. </s>
<s> There is really no cost effective solution for a end consumer to really find out how radio waves are passing though their house. </s>
<s> As a last resort if you cannot live with the speed is to go to all N equipment. </s>
<s> The manufactures try to sell N as being so much faster with better coverage etc etc. </s>
<s> The key benefit to N is that it tolerates interference better which leads to better coverage and faster throughput. </s>
<s> You will need N devices on all your equipment or everything will revert to G and you will get no benefit. </s>
<s> But again does this really impact your ability to use your systems. </s>
<s> Unless you are doing machine to machine traffic your internet will be the bottleneck. </s>
