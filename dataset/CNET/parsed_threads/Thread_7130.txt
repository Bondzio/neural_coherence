<s> A shot in the dark, but three things I can think of: 1. </s>
<s> The humming sound you hear may be a failing processor fan. </s>
<s> 2. </s>
<s> Another possibility is that power connector's wires touching the fan blade and thus not allowing the fan to reach its full RPM. </s>
<s> 3. </s>
<s> You might check to see if the processor fan has come loose during your move. </s>
<s> This would keep the fan from making the proper contact with the processor core. </s>
<s> In all three scenarios, if the processor fan is not working properly, your processor will overheat. </s>
<s> I can't remember if the Pentium III has over-heating (thermal) protection or not, but if it does, it will reduce its speed from the full 2800 MHz to &lt;literally&gt; a crawl. </s>
<s> If you are experiencing crashing problems, I'm pretty sure that your proc is overheating. </s>
<s> You might make sure the memory modules are seated properly. </s>

<s> Other thoughts: You should check your BIOS settings to make sure that the processor's multiplier and front-side bus speed are being reported correctly. </s>
<s> While in the BIOS, check for a menu item on the main screen called &quot;Temperature Settings&quot; or &quot;PC Health&quot;. </s>
<s> You might have a boot virus--although I don't think that would cause your PC to load for 7 minutes. </s>
<s> ~~~~~~~~~ If that's not the cause, we can go back to the fan issue. </s>
<s> Your PC seems more than a few years old so we may be on the right track. </s>
<s> To check your fans: While the PC is off: - Ground yourself to the PC case and at least make sure you don't shock anything when you touch it. </s>
<s> Static electricity is not your friend. </s>
<s> - Open the case - Your fans are probably covered with dust. </s>
<s> Check the proc fan and chipset fans if you have them. </s>
<s> Also check the case fans for dust. </s>
<s> If dusty, go to Wal-Mart and get a can of compressed air and blow it out. </s>
<s> DO NOT shake the can of air WHILE blowing out the case. </s>
<s> Keep it as still as possible so the liquid doesn't spray out. </s>
<s> - Verify that the fans are all seated properly. </s>
<s> You can just lightly wiggle them left and right, pushing and pulling. </s>
<s> - Reseat the memory modules if you feel comfortable with doing that. </s>
<s> Handle them by the module's PCB edges and not the chips. </s>
<s> They should snap in place and only fit one way. </s>
<s> If all fans seem to be seated properly, turn on the PC: - Watch and listen for the fans to spin up. </s>
<s> Processor fans spin very quickly--usually upwards of 5400 rpm to 7200 rpm. </s>
<s> If the processor fan is slow to spin up, makes a squeaking noise, an annoying buzzing noise that doesn't *seem* normal, or seems to vary in speed from slow to fast, fast to slow (and repeats this), you should replace it. </s>
<s> Your fan should screw into your processor's heatsink somehow so it should be easy to replace. </s>
<s> Just remember that this MAY NOT BE the cause of your XP slow down problems!!! </s>
<s> If you want to proceed, you can get fans for around $10 - 20 at PC parts stores, zipzoomfly.com, etc. </s>
<s> Just make sure to get a fan that is the same size as your heatsink. </s>
<s> Or, you may find a fan/heatsink combo that fits on your processor. </s>
<s> If you get one of these, you'll have to remove the old heatsink, fan, and old thermal paste from the processor. </s>
<s> If you don't know how to replace it, look on tomshardware.com for tutorials on how to do this. </s>
<s> Or, you might find a friend with experience, or take it to a repair shop. </s>
<s> If all else fails, you should back up any data you have and do a clean install of XP. </s>
