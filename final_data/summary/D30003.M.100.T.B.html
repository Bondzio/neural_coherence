British authorities arrested General Augusto Pinochet , in London for back surgery , on an international warrant issued by Spanish magistrate Baltasar Garzon . The Madrid court charged Pinochet , who ruled Chile as a despot for 17 years , with crimes against humanity , including genocide and terrorism , involving the deaths of more than 4,000 people . The Chilean government protested that Pinochet , now a `` senator-for-life , '' has legal immunity , but few in Chilean society protested the arrest . Pinochet 's arrest shows the growing significance of international human-rights law , suggesting that officials accused of atrocities have fewer places to hide these days , even if they are carrying diplomatic passports .
