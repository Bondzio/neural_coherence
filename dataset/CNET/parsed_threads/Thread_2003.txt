<s> It is very likely that a computer with those specifications can only take a PATA drive and not a SATA drive (without adding a PCI controller card). </s>
<s> So you need to get an ATA/100 or ATA/133 drive. </s>
<s> The lowest capacity drives today are usually 80GB. </s>
<s> You will find some 40GB drives, but there is little price difference between them and the 80GB models. </s>
<s> Check your local store websites for the rebate special HD of the week. </s>
<s> You can get over 100GB for less than $50 USD. </s>
<s> Note that there is a BIOS and operating system support issue for drives over 127GB and 138GB. </s>
<s> See http://www.48bitlba.com . </s>
<s> So you should be safe with a 120GB or smaller PATA drive. </s>
<s> More information on size issues: http://www.seagate.com/support/kb/disc/capacity/index.html </s>
<s> If you find a 40gb, great, but usually all too often 80gb and larger are being sold at sale prices quite often. </s>
<s> Any 40gb may not be new(be sure) as rebuilds or refurb units are being sold in that capacity. </s>
<s> I feel you may be better served buying a 80gb or better HD and feel your system can support it. </s>
<s> If 80gb+, you can partition it into 2-40gb (virtual) 40gb HDs to suit even further your 40gb needs. </s>
<s> In most cases, when buying a new HD buy the *HD kit*, that way you get all the goodies for a new HD install. </s>
<s> So-called OEM or whitebox HDs usually lack the small goodies that make a new HD harder to install. </s>
<s> Though you can get the same s/w via the HD support website but not the little h/w. </s>
<s> The cost is worth it and in a sale is a real plus. </s>
<s> Last, take my advice once the data or required data is transfered to new HD, remove the old HD and store it. </s>
<s> Lessen any chance that dual HDs will put excessive power demands on your old system. </s>
<s> Another reason to get a more than 40gb+ HD as you really don't need those 13gb no matter how attached to it, the 40gb+ will fill all your needs. </s>
