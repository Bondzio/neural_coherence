<s> &quot;If I set ICS to share it drops the internet connection.&quot; when a DHCP enabled router is being used. </s>
<s> Either the router distributes the LANs IP addresses or ICS does, not both. </s>
<s> I see that you are trying to share your internet connection from your first box to your two laptops. </s>
<s> I'm assuming you have windows xp and you're using a dsl router/gateway that assigns an address that says &quot;192.168.0.x&quot; and if that's the case, then you must realize that windows, by default, also assigns a &quot;192.168.0.x&quot; address when you enable ICS. </s>
<s> This will not work causing the two networks conflict with eachother. </s>
<s> You may solve this problem by changing the address on the dsl router/gateway to &quot;192.168.1.x&quot; but it will make your first &quot;box&quot; a part of the &quot;192.168.1.x&quot; network and all the other computers a part of the &quot;192.168.0.x&quot; thus eliminating the ability of the computer using ICS to share files with the other computers. </s>
<s> Since you have an access point, you don't have to eliminate your first box from sharing files to your other computers. </s>
<s> If you have a dsl router/gateway or cable modem with only one ethernet port, you can connect it to the internet/WAN side of your wireless router - I call it a router because you say you can enable DHCP on it. </s>
<s> Be sure to change the IP address on the wireless router so that it's not the same as the dsl/cable modem kinda like the addresses above. </s>
<s> Once you have that established, none of your computers need to be plugged into wireless router and all should have access to the internet via the wireless router. </s>
<s> If it's truely just a plain old access point with no routing capabilities, then plug the ethernet port on the dsl/cable modem to the (it probably only has one if it's just an AP) ethernet port on the access point and you'll get DHCP from the dsl/cable modem. </s>
<s> hope that helped. </s>
