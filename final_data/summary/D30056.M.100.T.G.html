Unknown Chechen gunmen abducted three Britons and one New Zealander , all employees of a British telephone company in the capitol city of Grozny in Chechnya . The British Foreign Office said it had warned the company to withdraw from Chechnya . More than 100 people are being held captive in Chechnya and anti-kidnapping officials have become victims of apparent hostage takers . Some two months after the kidnapping , Chechen officials reported finding the severed heads of the four kidnapped men near a small village west of Grozny . The Chechen president said the bodies of the four men were recovered but there was no further comment .
