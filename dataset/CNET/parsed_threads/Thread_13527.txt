<s> How much RAM are you shooting for ? </s>
<s> Of the software you are using or may use in the future which softwares system requirements are the most demanding? </s>
<s> Once you determine that then these requirements would be your new computers minimum system requirements. </s>
<s> A software program has a footprint of resources it uses so if you are using more than 1 program at a time your new computer will need to walk with all its feet. </s>
<s> The point is you need to figure out what your new computers total system requirements will be. </s>
<s> The software becomes your guide as to what you build. </s>
<s> By 2 monitors do you mean you want a continuous desktop or 2 monitors displaying the same thing? </s>
<s> Because your are running so many apps at the same time do you feel you need a bigger desktop? </s>
<s> If that is the case you need to pick a video card that can do a continuous desktop. </s>
<s> Then make sure the card comes with connections that will hook to the monitors you want to use. </s>
<s> The resolution and refresh rate of those monitors and video card must be high enough to allow you to work comfortably. </s>
<s> Because you use your current computer for work can you afford to scrap it for parts to build the new one? </s>
<s> I would recommend keeping it in a fully functional state while you build your new computer. </s>
<s> I often find that during a build I need to get drivers, check specs, and get advice/support. </s>
<s> So having a working computer around is very handy. </s>
<s> The perfect PC is just a compromise of what you want and what you can get. </s>
