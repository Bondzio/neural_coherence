<s> Duane </s>
<s> Observe the fans when you turn on the PC. </s>
<s> IF they just twitch and stop, the PS has tried to start but has shut down. </s>
<s> This is usually a detected overload. </s>
<s> It could be in the PC or the PS. </s>
<s> Disconnecting the PS from the PC will not help troubleshoot it. </s>
<s> You cannot easily start an ATX supply without it being connected to a load. </s>
<s> Even if you can jump the start switch connectors, it will refuse to start if no load is attached. </s>
<s> If the above symptom is true, strip the MB connections of add in cards, RAM CPU etc. </s>
<s> and disconnect the drives. </s>
<s> Turn on the PC and see if the fans will run. </s>
<s> If so, the PS has started. </s>
<s> You can measure voltages if you can find a diagram showing where to read them. </s>
<s> If the PS starts, you can reconnect things to see if it continues to shut down. </s>
<s> If it does, the connected device might be bad or the PS has reached its load limit. </s>
<s> If the PS never starts even with just the MB attached, you are probably looking at a bad MB or PS. </s>
<s> It's a matter of logically ruling things out. </s>
<s> Good luck. </s>
<s> with real thin/sharp test leads, you can probe the voltages from the rear of the molex connector on the mobo from the power supply. </s>
<s> The voltages of interest are the Main 5 volts, the 3.3 volts, and the +12 volts. </s>
<s> The minus voltages are of no interest. </s>
<s> The 5 volt standby voltage should be there anytime that the power supply is plugged into a live outlet [and the switch on the rear of the supply is on]. </s>
<s> This voltage is what allows the logic circuits to turn on the main voltages by a momentary contact power switch. </s>
