<s> With the CD drive out of action, your options are limited to enter any s/w? </s>
<s> I suggest you at least verify the USB ports are active in the bios area. </s>
<s> Once checked, get your hands on a registry cleaner for windows, that's XP capable, run it and reboot after its done its job. </s>
<s> That will clear any registry flags, etc., and hopefully return to a more pristine registry. </s>
<s> If the USB ports fail still, unplug all, and make sure if vailable to use a PS/2 mouse port for a mouse instead of USB. </s>
<s> You may need to enter bios again and enter PS/2 for mouse usage. </s>
<s> If it still fails you may have a h/w fault with the USB ports and no end-user is applicable other than replace mtrbd.. </s>
<s> The last option if not a h/w fault, then reload the OS as others offered to restart fresh, this will prove either a h/w or s/w(OS) fault if it fails to function. </s>
<s> The only other option as I see it, you may have a flaky power link to USB ports again possible mtrbd. </s>
<s> h/w fault(wiring) or chips overheated and are done in. </s>
