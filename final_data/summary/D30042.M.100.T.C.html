Two Libyans , accused by the United States and Britain of bombing a New York bound Pan Am jet over Lockerbie , Scotland in 1988 , killing 270 people , for 10 years were harbored by Libya who claimed the suspects could not get a fair trail in America or Britain . The U.N. imposed international air travel sanctions on Libya to force their extradition . The sanctions were honored by all but certain African countries . In 1998 , a U.S.-Britain brokered compromise offered that their trial to be held in the Netherlands . Gadhafi , tired of sanctions and hoping for increased tourism , actively pursued this compromise with UN Chief Kofi Annan .
