<s> Your hardware is likely to be prefectly able to run Windows XP and the apps you require, and it's likely that XP has been 'bogged down' over time. </s>
<s> I would address this by purchasing a new hard drive, and installing Windows XP onto this drive (formatted as NTFS). </s>
<s> Visit the Windows Update site and apply all of the high-priority updates. </s>
<s> You should notice that your system is performing much better than it did previously. </s>
<s> Reinstall all the apps you require and also install basic anti-virus software. </s>
<s> I use a combination of AVG AV Free Edition and Windows Defender, which are both unintrusive and do not hog system resources. </s>
<s> In my opinion avoid Norton AV. </s>
<s> Connect your original hard drive as second drive and then you'll be able to access your original data. </s>
<s> You also have the option of booting from your original hard drive, if required, by changing the boot sequence in the BIOS. </s>
<s> Your system would then benefit from a RAM upgrade. </s>
<s> Add an additional 512Mb PC-2700 SDRAM and you should notice a further performace improvement, especially when you are running apps concurrently. </s>
<s> Address the task in small stages and don't attempt to bite off the project in one hit. </s>
<s> This way you can always back-track if required without fear of data loss. </s>
