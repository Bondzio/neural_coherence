<s> When you scan a document the resulting file is a graphic, not text. </s>
<s> If you scan the document using the OCR function, then you get a text file. </s>
<s> It sounds like the 38 pages were scanned as a single page, not individual graphics, thus you get one long graphic. </s>
<s> I have used my HP multi-function printer to scan documents and have selected either the graphic output, or the text output. </s>
<s> If you select PDF output, it is still going to output the pages as graphic captures and not text. </s>
<s> The resolution you set for the scan will also affect the size of the output file in bytes and in inches. </s>
<s> NIL </s>
<s> make sure that your zoom level is at a acceptable aspect ratio in comparison with your screen resolution and the PDF </s>
<s> HP scanning software often defaults to a 24 bit color mode, even if you're scanning black and white documents. </s>
<s> The scanner and its software can't determine what you're scanning. </s>
<s> You have to set the requirements yourself. </s>
<s> If you just want to scan black &amp; white text documents, there should be a setting in your scanning software that is labeled as such i.e., &quot;black &amp; white,&quot; or just, &quot;text.&quot; Avoid settings for, &quot;256 shades of gray,&quot; unless you need it for some kind of black and white image, and avoid the OCR option unless you actually need to attempt text editing on the final document. </s>
<s> OCR recognition will only work well if you're scanning very legible text and it will also add to the file size of the finished document. </s>
<s> Most of this should be in the Help menus of scanning software. </s>
<s> There should also be a setting in your scanning software to control the page size. </s>
<s> It may default to the maximum size of the scanner bed, but you need to tell it the size of your scanned document. </s>
