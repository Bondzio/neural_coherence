<s> Question 1 - Why does the Hard Drive need to be partitioned and formatted? </s>
<s> Answer: Because no operating system works with an unpartitioned and unformatted HDD...even if it's only a single HDD with a single partition. </s>
<s> Question 2 - How is partitioning and formatting done? </s>
<s> I read in one particular CNET response, that all you had to do in Windows was to go to &quot;Disk Management&quot; to complete this task, however, I have searched and can not even find &quot;Disk Management&quot; on my computer. </s>
<s> Go to: Start Settings Control Panel Administrative Tools Computer Mgmt Disk Mgmt </s>
<s> Partitioning is like dividing your hard disk into sections, and will show up as different drive letters in your file manager. </s>
<s> So, you could set up a single hard disk with 2 partitions, one being the c:/ drive and the other as d:/ drive. </s>
<s> There are multiple reasons for doing this. </s>
<s> One reason is to help you manage your files better. </s>
<s> Your OS would reside on the c:/ partition, and you could keep personal files on the d:/ drive. </s>
<s> This could also make the drive more efficient by keeping the OS files separate from the clutter of other programs or files. </s>
<s> Partitioning is also required if you want to have a dual-boot computer. </s>
<s> If you wanted to be able to boot to either Windows XP or Linux, then each OS would have to reside on its own partition. </s>
<s> Formatting is done after partitioning. </s>
<s> This basically sets up how your computer will organize files on the partition. </s>
